I am a mythical bird who exists to bring good luck to KU. Hover your mouse over me for goodluck! The term "Jayhawk" was originally a term used by pro-slavery Kansans to describe Free State supporters during the "Bleeding Kansas" period in the 1850s. Over time, "Jayhawk" lost its war-like connotations and Kansans came to embrace the title as a term of endearment. My name came from two birds common to the area, the sparrow hawk and the bluejay. I became the mascot for the University when Eureka, Kansas student Henry Maloy created the first cartoon in 1912.
